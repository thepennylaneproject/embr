## When to Raise Money

### Option A: Never Raise (Bootstrap Forever)

```
Pros:
✓ Keep 100% control
✓ Forced to be efficient
✓ True to DIY mission
✓ No investor pressure

Cons:
✗ Slower feature development
✗ Limited team (can only hire what revenue supports)
✗ More pressure on you personally
✗ Competitors with funding will move faster

Feasible? Yes, if you're patient.
Timeline: 2-3 years to meaningful scale.
```

### Option B: Raise Small Round ($100-150k) at Month 9

```
Timing:
├─ Month 9: $22k/month revenue, 850 creators
├─ Proof of concept works
├─ Ready to accelerate
└─ Raise $100-150k

Use of funds:
├─ Your salary: $6,000/month × 18 months = $108k
├─ First engineer: $4,000/month × 18 months = $72k
├─ Second engineer: $4,000/month × 12 months = $48k
├─ Buffer: $22k
└─ Total needed: $250k

Reality: Raise $150k, stretch it 12 months, hit break-even.

Terms (Recommended):
├─ SAFE note or convertible
├─ $10-15M valuation (don't give away too much equity)
├─ 20-30% equity total to all investors (not you)
├─ Founder friendly terms (board seat, veto rights)

Pros:
✓ Hire full team immediately
✓ Scale fast (2,000+ creators by month 12)
✓ Build features faster
✓ Reduce personal financial pressure

Cons:
✗ Investors expect growth metrics
✗ Pressure to optimize for user growth (not creator earnings)
✗ Complicated cap table
✗ Future decisions require investor approval

Recommended? Maybe. Only if you find investors who get the mission.
```

### My Recommendation: Option A → Option B

**Year 1: Bootstrap only**
- Live frugally for months 1-3
- Hire freelancer month 4-5 ($2k/mo)
- Hire first full-time engineer month 6-7 ($4k/mo)
- You take $4k/month salary by month 6

**Month 9-10: Re-evaluate**
- If growth is solid (500+ creators, $20k+/month revenue)
- Consider small raise ($100-150k) to scale faster
- If growth is slow, keep bootstrapping
- If you're burned out, definitely raise

**By Month 12:**
- Either sustainable on platform revenue alone
- Or have raised $100-150k and on path to $100k+ monthly revenue

## Transparency: What You Tell Creators

### "Why 2% Platform Fee?"

```
HOW YOUR MONEY FLOWS ON EMBR

You earn: $1,000
Payment processor: -3% = -$30 (standard for all platforms)
Embr platform fee: -2% = -$20
You receive: $950

Total cut taken: 5%

HOW THAT 2% BREAKS DOWN

$1,000 × 2% = $20 from one creator
× 1,000 creators
= $20,000/month platform revenue

Where it goes:
├─ Servers & hosting: $1,500 (7%)
├─ Payment processing: $1,500 (7%)
├─ Salaries (You + engineers): $12,500 (63%)
├─ Legal/compliance: $250 (1%)
├─ Reinvestment (new features): $4,250 (22%)
└─ Total: $20,000

COMPARISON

Patreon:  8% fee + 2.2% payment processing = 10.2%
Substack: 10% fee (on top of payment processor)
YouTube:  45% (they take roughly 45%, you get 55%)
Spotify:  70% (you get ~30%)
Apple Podcasts: 30%

Embr at 5% total is among the lowest out there.
And you know exactly where your 2% goes.

We publish these numbers publicly every quarter.
```

## Risk Factors & Mitigation

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|
| Slow creator adoption | Launch with warm network, do outreach, seek journalists/historians early |
| High churn | Focus on creating revenue ASAP (make it worthwhile to stay) |
| Creators earn less than $1,300/mo avg | Either get bigger creators or more of them (adjust pricing later if needed) |
| You burn out | Hire first person by month 5, not month 6 |
| Unexpected costs | Keep 3-month buffer (month 3-6 don't spend all revenue) |
| Competitors copy | Build moat through community, transparency, governance (harder to copy) |
